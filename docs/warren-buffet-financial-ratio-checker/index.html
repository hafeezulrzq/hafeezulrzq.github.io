About This Tool

This tool evaluates companies based on Warren Buffett's investment principles. Enter the financial ratios below, and we'll analyze whether the company meets Buffett's criteria for a quality investment. The analysis considers factors like profitability, debt levels, valuation, and financial stability.

Financial Ratios Evaluated

This tool evaluates companies based on the following ratios, each contributing to the overall investment score:

1. Return on Equity (ROE) - 15 points

Measures how effectively a company uses shareholders’ equity. Buffett prefers companies with ROE consistently above 15%.

2. Debt-to-Equity Ratio - 15 points

Lower debt means less financial risk. Buffett prefers companies with debt-to-equity ratio below 0.5.

3. Current Ratio - 10 points

Measures short-term liquidity. A ratio above 1.5 indicates good ability to cover short-term obligations.

4. Net Profit Margin - 15 points

Shows profitability per dollar of revenue. Higher and consistent margins are preferred.

5. Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio - 10 points

Valuation metric. Buffett prefers P/E ratios below 20, though this varies by industry.

6. Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio - 10 points

Compares market value to book value. A P/B ratio below 3 is generally considered reasonable.

7. Earnings Growth Rate - 10 points

Consistent earnings growth indicates a strong business. Buffett looks for steady, predictable growth.

8. Free Cash Flow - 10 points

Positive and growing free cash flow indicates financial health and ability to reinvest or return capital to shareholders.

Total Possible Score: 100 points

Rating System

  • 80-100%: Excellent Rating According to Warren Buffett Principles
  • 65-79%: Good Rating According to Warren Buffett Principles
  • 50-64%: Moderate Rating According to Warren Buffett Principles
  • Below 50%: Poor Rating According to Warren Buffett Principles

The tool evaluates businesses based on how well they align with Warren Buffett’s investment philosophy, focusing on long-term value, financial stability, and sustainable competitive advantages.
